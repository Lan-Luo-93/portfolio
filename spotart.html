Spot Art

UI & UX DESIGN

SpotArt is a street art discovery app providing immersive experiences for visitors, while allowing the artists’ artwork to evolve and empowering their creative expression, using augmented reality.

Overview

Challenge

San Francisco is one of the top cities to experience murals in the United States. People enjoy viewing street art but sometimes they don’t understand what they see due to lack of information, and some murals might even be difficult to find. This might result in artists not receiving the exposure they deserve. Visitors need a way to engage with street art because their current engagement is too quick and shallow.

Solution

I believe that by creating an AR app that allows visitors to discover and interact with murals, the audience will achieve a higher satisfaction level, and artists are also provided with an opportunity to create content beyond the traditional format.

Bring street art to life with SpotArt

SpotArt puts an endlessly expressive story-telling tool into the hands of artists and into the lives of the audience.

Experience murals with sound, motion and 3D elements

Discover nearby artworks on a map effortlessly. Point your camera towards the artwork and see curated content in AR created by muralists.

Go on immersive street art audio tours

Listen to insightful stories about artworks and their communities while following pre-set walking routes. Put on headphones, tap start, and the audio will play at the right time and place.

Promote artwork better by adding AR content

Simply scan and tap to place digital elements to your work. Take your audience on a journey through art. Your artwork has the power to evolve.

1. User Research

Ethnographic Research

I did a field trip to gain insight into people’s habits and observe how people in public interact with street art. It was a sunny day; perfect weather for a site visit to Clarion Alley and Balmy Alley.

Observations

• Spectators viewed the art for a small amount of time (1–2 minutes).

• Spectators took pictures and were posing in front of the art but left immediately after.

• No information about the art piece was located nearby.

Competitive Analysis

After the site visit I decided to conduct an analysis among apps and sites that promote street art and art exhibitions. I compared the difference and similarities between them, both visually and functionally. This analysis helped me understand and identify both the problems and areas to focus on.

Key Findings

• All apps featured an interactive map and points of interest to be visited

• All apps had a database with information about artists and artworks

• There were no pre-set walking paths and audio tours for viewing street art

• There is still a limited number of VR/AR apps that enhances the street art viewing experience

User interviews

To understand the specific features which users need, I posted a screener survey and conducted interviews with some relevant users:

Insights

• Users unintentionally run into street/public art.

• Users want information about public art, but don’t know where to look.

• Most users do not research the art further online/onsite.

• Users take pics and post to social media.

• Users would interrupt their current routine to take a picture.

• Most users think an audio guide would benefit the viewing experience.

User Personas

Through research, I learned that people visit murals for different reasons. Some visit to take photos for the social aspect, some are interested in art, and others want to learn about the community stories. With these findings I identified three types of target users.
