FestShare

UI & UX DESIGN

FestShare is a clothing exchange app which focuses on fostering local social connections between music festival lovers through trading garments while helps people minimize the amount of waste they produce.

Challenge

Excessive clothing waste. I learned that there is a substantial amount of waste coming from single-use fashion for music festivals and events. The colorful, occasionally, and wildly creative outfits that attendees wear can cost a lot of money. A report found that single-use outfits for music festivals, such as Glastonbury and Coachella, alone, account for approximately $307 million worth of items per year, or about 7.5 million outfits worn only once.

SWIPE TO MATCH ITEMS WITH OTHERS IN LOCAL AREA

Trade the clothes you have for the clothes you want. Variety of fashionable items to fit your needs.

QUICK AND EASY LISTING PROCESS

Upload your pre-loved clothes and get matches easily. Everything is free, and all trades are done in person.

CHAT, MEET UP AND TRADE YOUR CLOTHES

A built-in messaging system to chat and set up a trade with fashion enthusiasts nearby. Expand your social networks effortlessly.

User Interviews

I conducted interviews with 12 people who had previously been to music festivals or had experiences using clothing resale apps and websites. Here are some of the key take aways from those results:

Key Learnings

1. Users did utilize online marketplaces and valued them as source to get rid of items they no longer needed, as well as to find the things they want.

2. Being able to trade easily and within a convenient timeframe was a priority.

3. Safety when conducting exchanges was of an utmost importance.

Target Audience

As I analyzed the interview insights, it became clear that this product is meant to appeal to young people in their twenties, a market of young adults with little disposable income looking to find new ways of expressing themselves.

User Persona

Based on the information above, I developed my user persona, a representation of the target audience that helps me keep users’ goals, needs, and pain points at the front of my minds throughout the design process.

    • Naomi is a college student who is socially active and likes going to music festivals.

    Goals

    • To be more sustainable, find a convienient and efficient solution to disposing festival outfits that she doesn’t need.

    • To connect people who interested in music festivals and fashion

    Pain points

    • Spend too much money on clothing for special occasions and events around 3–4 times a year.

Wireframe

I started wireframing and conduct informal user tests. A few competitors I drew inspiration from include OfferUp and Depop. OfferUp is a marketplace focus on quick local exchanges and Depop, a clothing resale app also promotes a heavy social aspect.
